# Decision & Approval Process

AIEF employs a structured process to capture decisions, enforce accountability, and maintain traceability.

1. **Decision Identification**
   - When a trade‑off or design choice arises, an *AI Architect* drafts an ADR candidate. 

2. **Review & Rationale**
   - The team reviews the ADR for clarity, scope, and impact.  
   - Feedback is collected via comment threads; revisions are made until consensus.

3. **Approval**
   - A designated decision owner (usually a senior engineer or product lead) gives formal approval.  
   - Approval is recorded in version control alongside the ADR.

4. **Documentation & Propagation**
   - Approved ADRs are linked from relevant artifacts (e.g., design docs, code modules).  
   - The AI *Project Manager* updates status dashboards and triggers any necessary re‑testing or refactoring.

5. **Governance**
   - All decisions are archived in the `docs/adr` directory.  
   - A quarterly audit reviews ADR compliance with organizational policies.

This process ensures that every architectural change is deliberate, justified, and traceable from requirement to deployment.
